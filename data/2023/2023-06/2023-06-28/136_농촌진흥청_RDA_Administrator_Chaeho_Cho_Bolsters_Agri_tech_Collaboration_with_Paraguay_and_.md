# RDA Administrator Chaeho Cho Bolsters Agri-tech Collaboration with Paraguay and Uruguay

- 부처: 농촌진흥청
- 배포일: 06/28/2023 16:00:00
- NewsItemId: 156577572
- 원문: https://www.korea.kr/briefing/pressReleaseView.do?newsId=156577572&call_from=openData

## 본문

The Rural Development Administration (RDA), under the leadership of Administrator Chaeho Cho, has announced it hosted bilateral meetings in Asunción, Paraguay's capital, on June 20-21. The primary aim was to strengthen agricultural technology cooperation and solicit support for Korea’s bid to host the 2030 Busan World Expo. Moreover, on June 22, in Montevideo, Uruguay's capital, an MOU on agri-tech collaboration was concluded with Uruguay's Office of Planning and Budget and the Ministry of Livestock, Agriculture, and Fisheries of the Oriental Republic of Uruguay.

Administrator Chaeho Cho (hereinafter referred to as Administrator Cho) had a productive dialogue with Moises Santiago Bertoni Hicar, Paraguay's Minister of Agriculture and Livestock. They discussed the outcome of Official Development Assistance (ODA) on agricultural technology and future bilateral collaboration. Paraguay aspires to deepen its agri-tech cooperation with Korea to support small-scale farmers, advance self-reliance in primary food crops, and boost exports of prime Paraguayan produce like beef, corn, and sesame seeds to Korea. Administrator Cho also had a meeting with Irineo Raúl Silvero Silvagni, the Vice Minister of Foreign Affairs of Paraguay, introducing the status of the agri-tech cooperation between the two nations and seeking Paraguay's endorsement for the 2030 Busan World Expo.

Administrator Cho also took part in the International Symposium on the Development of the Seed Potato Industry in Latin America, held at the Gran Bourbon Asunción Hotel. The symposium gathered over 30 attendees, including the Paraguayan Minister of Agriculture and Livestock, the President of the Paraguayan Institute of Agriculture Technology, and Chan-sik Yoon, the Korean Ambassador to Paraguay. During this symposium, the results of seed potato projects operating in six KOPIA member countries in Latin America were shared, and challenges and future direction related to the projects were also discussed.* 6 countries: Paraguay, Bolivia, Ecuador, Nicaragua, Guatemala, Dominican Republic

Subsequently, Administrator Cho journeyed to Uruguay, where a tripartite MOU was signed with Issac Alfie, Director of the Office of Planning and Budget, and Fernando Mattos, Minister of Agriculture, Livestock, and Fisheries. Eun-chul Lee, the Korean Ambassador to Uruguay and several Uruguayan officials attended the signing ceremony, which was designed to foster agri-tech collaboration for the development of bio-green industries in both countries.

The MOU highlights the joint lab establishment, joint research, and experts exchange. This MOU follows the Director of the Office of Planning and Budget’s call for agri-tech collaboration during his visit to RDA in September 2022.

"Strengthening cooperation in the field of seed potato production technology is critical to addressing food security in Latin American countries," said Administrator Cho. "The signing of the Korea-Uruguay Memorandum of Understanding on Agricultural Technology Cooperation will serve as a bridgehead for further cooperation between the two countries." He said.
