# Rural Development Administration Hosted Greenhouse Gas Emission Reduction Forum in Latin American Agricultural Sector

- 부처: 농촌진흥청
- 배포일: 06/12/2023 00:00:00
- NewsItemId: 156574572
- 원문: https://www.korea.kr/briefing/pressReleaseView.do?newsId=156574572&call_from=openData

## 본문

The Rural Development Administration (RDA) has jointly organize a two-day forum on Greenhouse Gas (GHG) Emission Reduction in the agricultural sector with the International Center for Tropical Agriculture (CIAT1)) in Cali, Colombia on June 5th and 6th. The event focused on addressing GHG emissions in the agricultural sector of various Latin American countries.

1) CIAT (Centro Internacional de Agricultura Tropical): A nonprofit research institution under the Consultative Group for International Agriculture Research (CGIAR), which emphasizes scientific research and development to address agricultural issues in tropical regions.

 This forum was organized with the aim of exploring sustainable approaches to GHG emission reduction and foster agricultural technology cooperation between Korea and Latin America. On the first day of the forum, which brought together 60 green house researchers and officials from six Latin American countries, the main agenda focused on discussing the current status of GHG Inventories and responses to GHG emission by each country.

 Participants shared policies, long-term strategies and ongoing research related to reducing GHG emission reduction in Latin America. Dr. Jeong Hyun-cheol researcher at the National Institute of Agricultural Science, delivered a presentation on the current status of GHG research in the Korean agricultural sector. He introduced the Korean government’s GHG reduction policies, efforts and mitigation technologies.

 On the second day, initiatives based in Latin America such as AgriLAC and MITIGATE+, presented cases of transitioning to climate-resilient agricultural production and research on improving food systems to reduce GHG emissions in developing countries.

 The RDA of Republic of Korea is actively promoting the Korea-Latin America Food & Agriculture Cooperation Initiative (KoLFACI2)). As part of this initiative, a research project titled ‘Research on the optimal cultivation methods to reduce greenhouse gas emissions in Latin-America’ is being conducted with the participation of five countries: Guatemala, El Salvador, Nicaragua, Honduras, and Colombia.

 2)KoLFACI (Korea-Latin America Food & Agriculture Cooperation Initiative): Multi-lateral cooperative body under the RDA, aimed to addressing agricultural challenges in Latin America through joint project, with the goals of ensuring food security and bridging the technological gap.

 Kim Hwang-yong, the Director of International Technology Cooperation Center at the RDA stated, “Amid the urgent need for intergovernmental action to reduce GHG emissions and effectively address climate change, these collaborative efforts and cooperation will serve as a foundation for more countries to participate in resolving climate change issues.”
